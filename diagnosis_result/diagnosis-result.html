Tomato Blight

1 Summary of Diagnosis

  • Disease Identified: Late Blight
  • Affected Plant: Tomato
  • Symptoms: The leaves show dark brown spots with a water-soaked appearance, and some areas have turned yellow and brown. The stems may also have lesions, and the fruit could show dark, greasy spots.

2 Main Cause of the Problem

  • Pathogen: The disease is caused by the fungus-like organism Phytophthora infestans. It thrives in cool, wet conditions, and spreads quickly through water, wind, or direct contact with infected plants.

3 Temporary Solution

  • Immediate Action: Remove and destroy all infected leaves and fruit to prevent further spread. Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation. Avoid overhead watering to keep foliage dry.
  • Cultural Practices: Implement crop rotation and use resistant tomato varieties if available.

4 Recommended Products

  • Fungicides:
    • Copper fungicides can be applied as a preventive measure or at the first signs of the disease.
    • Chlorothalonil or Mancozeb are also effective options.
